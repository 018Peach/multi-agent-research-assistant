# Sample run — general research (text-only, energy domain)

Another **non-CX, text-only** run — a comparative "survey + compare" question that produces a
structured answer (including a comparison table), showing the system handles analytical breadth
across yet another domain.

---

## Input
> What are the leading grid-scale energy-storage technologies in 2026, and how do they compare on
> cost, maturity, and best use case?

## Output

In 2026, the grid-scale energy-storage market is characterized by a rapid expansion of annual
deployments, projected to reach 158 GW (excluding pumped hydro) (energy-storage.news). The landscape
is dominated by mature lithium-ion technologies, while newer chemistries and long-duration
mechanical systems are scaling to meet diverse grid needs.

**1. Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP)**
- *Maturity & market share:* LFP is the dominant battery technology, expected to hold a 74% market
  share by 2025/2026 (sciencedirect.com). It has a Technology Readiness Level (TRL) of 9 — a fully
  commercialized, mature solution (IEA).
- *Best use case:* Short-duration services (1–4 hours) including frequency regulation and peak
  shaving (astracanyon.com).

**2. Pumped Storage Hydropower (PSH)**
- *Maturity & market share:* A cornerstone of global capacity, projected to provide 42% of the
  expansion in electricity storage through 2026 (iea.org). Highly mature (TRL 9).
- *Cost:* Capital expenditures for 2026 estimated between $1,999 and $5,505 per kW (atb.nrel.gov).
- *Best use case:* Large-scale, multi-day storage and bulk power management (patsnap.com).

**3. Sodium-Ion Batteries**
- *Maturity:* Scaling rapidly in 2026 as a lower-cost alternative to lithium-ion
  (insightaceanalytic.com). Industrial viability is signalled by major agreements, such as the
  60 GWh deal between CATL and HyperStrong (energy-storage.news).
- *Best use case:* Short-duration stationary storage where lower cost is prioritized over high
  energy density (patsnap.com).

**4. Vanadium Redox Flow Batteries (VRFB)**
- *Maturity & market size:* The global flow-battery market is projected to reach $1.22 billion by
  2026 (fortunebusinessinsights.com).
- *Performance:* Superior cycle life (15,000–20,000 cycles) compared to lithium-ion
  (eureka.patsnap.com).
- *Best use case:* Daily energy shifting with discharge durations of 4–12 hours (patsnap.com).

**5. Long-Duration Energy Storage (LDES): Iron-Air Batteries**
- *Maturity:* Reaching TRL 7–8 and transitioning to multi-gigawatt utility scale by 2026 (IEA;
  enkiai.com). The broader LDES market is expected to surpass $6.34 billion in revenue by 2026
  (researchandmarkets.com).
- *Best use case:* Extreme long-duration needs — up to 100 hours of discharge for multi-day storage
  (enkiai.com).

**Summary comparison (2026)**

| Technology | Maturity (TRL) | Primary use case | Key advantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| LFP (Lithium) | 9 | Short-duration (1–4h) | Dominant market share (74%) |
| Sodium-Ion | 8 | Short-duration | Lower cost than Li-ion |
| Flow (VRFB) | 8–9 | Daily shifting (4–12h) | High cycle life (15k–20k) |
| Pumped Hydro | 9 | Multi-day storage | High capacity; established |
| Iron-Air | 7–8 | Multi-day (100h) | Ultra-long duration |
